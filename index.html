1) Which phenomenon clearly demonstrates the particle nature of light?

solution

Ans: C In the photoelectric effect, electrons are emitted from a metal surface only when light of sufficient frequency falls on it. This shows that light transfers energy in discrete packets (photons), which is a particle-like behavior and cannot be explained by classical wave theory.

2) The unit of Planck’s constant is:

solution

Ans: B From the formula E=hν, energy is measured in joules and frequency in hertz (s⁻¹). Therefore, the unit of Planck’s constant must be joule-second (J·s).

3) Who proposed that particles like electrons have wave nature?

solution

Ans: D Louis de Broglie suggested that not only light but all moving particles exhibit wave-like properties. This idea introduced the concept of matter waves and became a foundation of quantum mechanics.

4) What type of spectrum is emitted by a black body?

solution

Ans: C A black body emits radiation over a wide range of wavelengths without gaps. Hence, the emitted spectrum is continuous, unlike line or band spectra produced by gases.

5) What happens to the kinetic energy of photoelectrons when the frequency of incident light increases?

solution

Ans: C When the frequency of incident light increases, the energy of photons increases. After overcoming the work function of the metal, the extra energy appears as kinetic energy of emitted electrons, so their kinetic energy increases.

6) The de Broglie wavelength of a particle depends on:

solution

Ans: B The de Broglie wavelength is given by λ=m/h This shows that wavelength depends on the particle’s mass and velocity. Heavier or faster particles have shorter wavelengths.

7) Which experiment verified the wave nature of electrons?

solution

Ans: C In the Davisson–Germer experiment, electrons showed diffraction patterns when scattered by a crystal. Diffraction is a wave property, thus confirming the wave nature of electrons.

8) Threshold frequency refers to:

solution

Ans: B Threshold frequency is the minimum frequency of incident radiation required to just eject electrons from a metal surface. Below this frequency, photoelectric emission does not occur regardless of intensity.

9) Photoelectric emission does not occur when:

solution

Ans: B Even very intense light cannot eject electrons if its frequency is below the threshold frequency. Frequency, not intensity, determines whether photoelectric emission will occur.

10) Which particle has the largest de Broglie wavelength?

solution

Ans: A Since de Broglie wavelength is inversely proportional to mass, lighter particles like electrons have much larger wavelengths than heavier objects such as balls or cars.
